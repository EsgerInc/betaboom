# Elm AI AGM Deck v6 Talk Track

## 1. Cover
My name is Advait, and I’m the founder of Elm AI. We build AI agents for responsible sourcing and supplier operations.

## 2. A Fashion Brand Managing 600 Factories Worldwide
Start with a fashion brand managing around 600 factories across countries, product lines, and seasons. Coordination and supplier communication never stop. Tariffs, quality issues, and supplier risk change the plan constantly. But the delivery window still does not move.

## 3. Multiple Teams Have To Sign Off
Now zoom in on what it takes to start working with a supplier. Before a factory can actually start moving product, multiple internal teams need to sign off: compliance, legal, procurement, marketing, design, and responsible sourcing. A missing document, an open issue, or unclear ownership can hold everything up.

## 4. Responsible Sourcing Becomes the Operational Choke Point
Inside that sign-off process, responsible sourcing becomes the operational choke point. They sit at the intersection of compliance, procurement, and business motion. We kept hearing versions of the same sentence: we cannot source this product until due diligence is done. That is when a factory issue turns directly into a business issue.

## 5. The Real Bottleneck Was The Work After The Data Arrived
The important insight was that these teams did not lack data. They already had audits, supplier records, spreadsheets, trackers, systems of record, and document storage. The real bottleneck was the work after the information arrived: review, follow-up, routing, coordination, deadline tracking, and remediation.

## 6. This Is Why Elm Started With Audit Management And CAP Tracking
That is why we built Elm the way we did. Audit management and CAP tracking were the first wedge because the pain was immediate and the ROI was obvious. Companies were paying hundreds of dollars to review a single audit, internal teams were still underwater, and the work that mattered most was still not moving fast enough.

## 7. Elm Takes Audit Management, CAP Tracking, And Follow-Through Off The Team
For a 600-factory program, maybe only around 100 factories get deep review while the rest of the program keeps moving. Backlogs build fast. Scoring and CAP creation stay manual. Remediation gets squeezed out. Elm takes that workflow off the team by organizing audits, scoring findings, drafting CAPs, tracking follow-through, and keeping supplier follow-up live.

## 8. From Finding to Fix
Once the audit lands, the loop starts moving immediately. Findings get extracted, corrective actions get drafted, suppliers get engaged, and the deadline loop stays active. The result is that teams spend less time on paperwork and more time on actual remediation.

## 9. Even When Teams Are Offline, The Work Keeps Moving
Even within responsible sourcing, the product starts becoming an always-on operating layer. Work keeps moving even when no one is sitting in the product. Reviews get done, deadlines get watched, suppliers get nudged, and the program stays current across the day.

## 10. This Already Works In The Real World
This is already working with real customers. We have grown from 3 customers last year to more than 14 today. Revenue is up more than 5x year over year. We already have more than 8,000 factories on the platform. And the strongest traction today is in apparel and promotional goods.

## 11. The Market Is Starting To Pull
The category itself is also starting to pull. Recent Elm webinars and events have drawn hundreds of companies, including many of the largest brands across apparel, sourcing, and consumer products. The important point is not event attendance on its own. It is that major brands are actively looking for production AI in this category, and very few companies can point to real agents already in market. That is why Elm keeps getting pulled into the conversation.

## 12. We Can Build This Because We Know This World
This only works if you understand both the operational environment and what it takes to deploy AI in real enterprises. Our backgrounds span real-world AI systems, sustainability, supply chains, and operating programs inside major brands. That combination is what lets us build something that fits the actual work rather than just describing it.

## 13. We Have Already Helped Scale These Programs At Major Brands
Our team and our technology have helped build from scratch and scale responsible-sourcing programs across more than a dozen brands, many of them with more than a billion dollars in revenue. We understand the workflow pain, the internal coordination, and the enterprise constraints. That operating context is a big part of why we can start with one workflow and expand with credibility.

## 14. That Same System Can Extend Beyond Responsible Sourcing
That first workflow leads to the broader product idea. Elm runs each workflow on shared supplier context: status, owners, issues, deadlines, documents, and history. Inputs keep arriving from audits, supplier email, and deadlines, and the system already knows what happened, who owns the work, and what should happen next.

## 15. The Same Loop Can Run Supplier Operations
Once that context exists, the same loop extends beyond responsible sourcing. Quality holds, chargebacks, shipping delays, vendor scorecards, and supplier readiness all run through the same system. The supplier record stays live, the right owner gets pulled in, and the workflow keeps moving instead of disappearing into inboxes and handoffs.

## 16. When The Stakes Go Up, Elm Already Knows What To Do
When a serious issue appears, the company immediately needs context. Which supplier is affected? What factory? What orders are exposed? Who owns the response? How urgent is it? Elm connects that context, opens the response, and keeps it moving while there is still time to act.

## 17. Responsible Sourcing Is Where Elm Starts, Not Where It Ends
Responsible sourcing is where Elm starts, not where it ends. The same shared supplier record can support supplier operations, the broader supplier relationship, and eventually replace more of the legacy stack around supplier coordination and execution.

## 18. That Expansion Runs On One Live Supplier Record
At the center of that expansion is a live supplier record. One place that holds the supplier, the current blockers, the owner, the next deadline, the open issues, the documents, the performance history, and the current risk picture. That is the object the rest of the system runs on.

## 19. Once The Record Exists, You Can Deploy An Army Of Supply-Chain Agents
Once that shared record exists, you are no longer adding one-off features. You are deploying always-on supply-chain agents that run 24/7 against goals. Some manage audit findings and CAPs. Others track certificate renewals, supplier readiness, milestones, delays, scorecards, and risk. Over time, more of the supplier relationship gets handled by agents that keep performance moving even when teams are offline.

## 20. Elm Is Rebuilding The Supply Chain Management Stack For An Agent-First World
Elm is not just building a better workflow product for responsible sourcing. We are rebuilding the supply chain management stack for an agent-first world. It starts in responsible sourcing because that is where the pain is sharp and the ROI is immediate, expands across supplier operations, and over time aims to replace the fragmented legacy stack around supplier execution.
